      Overview
      Social AI uses a network of individual volunteers to create our LLM. Contributors sign up, making their personal computers and devices available when off-line. This gives us access to a vast resource of "free" computing power to build our AI. No need for the billion-dollar data center. Our software pushes out to these shared devices, managing them to crawl websites, scan documents, and generate content.

      What is Social AI?

      Social AI was founded in 2024. Our vision is to build a state-of-the-art Large Language Model AI, able to compete with the best current systems, without the massive expenditures on data centers which are now in commonplace.

      Our fundamental concept is to use a distributed, scalable network of individual computers and devices to perform necessary data acquisition and training tasks. Contributors — we call them Social Trainers — sign up voluntarily to make their computers available as part of the network. By doing so, they give our system access to those computers for building our LLM. 

      Contributor computers are only accessed when idle, so a Social Trainer is not impacted and can use their computer without interruption. All we ask is that the computer is left turned on when not in use, at least as much as is practicable.

      We plan to provide broad-spectrum AI services at very low cost — orders of magnitude less than what's out there now. 

      And while our Social Trainers are volunteers, we will reward them for their belief, confidence, and contribution. All registered users will recieve equity participation in the company in an appropriate manner when available.

      How does it work?

      When you join Social AI as a contributor, your computer or device will automatically become part of our network. Our software will "assign" tasks that your device will perform while it is idle. This will not impact your use of the computer. These tasks include such things as scraping websites for content, training on documents from the internet, and other data updates.

      You can also sign up from multiple devices, using the same or different email addresses. This will increase your financial benefit from participation. See the FAQ What's in it for me? for more information.

      In addition, while you are a much-appreciated volunteer, we will also reward you for your confidence and contribution. All registered users will recieve equity participation in the company in an appropriate manner when available.

      How do I participate as a Social Trainer?

      We are building our LLM using contributors like you, willing to make computers and other devices available for the technical tasks. To participate you can simply sign up as a Social Trainer. See the FAQ How do I sign up? for instructions.

      What is your process to build the LLM?

      Building a Large Language Model (LLM) involves several key steps:

      1. Define objectives and use case: Clearly identify the goals and specific tasks the LLM will perform[1][3].

      2. Prepare the dataset:
         - Collect diverse and relevant data from various sources
         - Clean and preprocess the data, removing irrelevant information and biases
         - Format the data consistently (e.g., JSON, CSV, or TXT)[1][2]

      3. Select the model architecture: Choose a framework like PyTorch or TensorFlow, and decide on an architecture (e.g., transformer-based models like GPT or BERT)[1][2].

      4. Build the model:
         - Define the model structure (layers, neurons, activation functions)
         - Implement the forward pass
         - Set up the loss function and optimizer[2]

      5. Train the model:
         - Pretrain on large general datasets
         - Fine-tune on task-specific datasets
         - Use learning rate schedulers and monitor validation loss[1]

      6. Evaluate and test:
         - Assess performance using metrics like perplexity, BLEU, or ROUGE scores
         - Benchmark against existing solutions
         - Test on real-world tasks[1]

      7. Deploy and monitor:
         - Choose a deployment option (e.g., cloud platforms)
         - Implement monitoring systems to track performance and model drift
         - Update and retrain periodically as new data becomes available[1]

      8. Hyperparameter tuning: Experiment with different configurations to optimize model performance[2].

      9. Implement text generation (if applicable): Use techniques like greedy decoding or beam search for generating text[2].

      Throughout the process, it's crucial to have access to sufficient computational resources, such as GPUs or TPUs, especially for training larger models[3].
